## Long tail statistics

When the probability of making an observing *x* outside the mean falls off by the square of the exponential of *x* then we call that a normal distribution. This is the every day (normal) experience we have about most things and a consequence of the central limit theorem. There is no intuition around other distributions and certainly when probabilities far from the central moment (the mean) become probable then intuition breaks down.
