04.01 Epistemic Bets

Epistemic Bets

Many organizations run as a management mix. A traditional control model can coexist with a complexity-informed digital unit in the same enterprise.

In this module, epistemic bets are the assumptions an organization makes about when it can know enough to decide.

In software-heavy work, activities happen in sequence, but understanding does not.

Words like discovery, exploration, and incremental delivery do not create learning on their own. Learning appears only when key assumptions are tested and decisions change with evidence.
