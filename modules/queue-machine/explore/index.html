How to read this lab

  1. Set load: tune arrivals and capacity to place the system in breathing room, strain, or near saturation.
  2. Add variation: raise arrival and service unevenness to see queue sensitivity.
  3. Check spill: watch local overflow create backlog even below 100% average utilization.
  4. Compare the readouts: M/M/1 assumes no variability; Kingman adds the full penalty from both arrival and service unevenness. The gap between the two is the cost of variability at your current load.
