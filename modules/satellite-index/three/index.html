Explorer

Pan to any agricultural area, zoom to field scale, and click Load indices — no boundary registration required. Seven Sentinel-2 spectral indices render as terrain above a satellite tile base fitted to the current bounds. Select an index to swap the surface; the imagery and camera position stay fixed. Orbit controls let you inspect from any angle.

NDVI — Vegetation density

Normalised red and near-infrared contrast. Useful for a first read of canopy density and broad vegetation vigour.

View on terrain ↑

NDRE — Red-edge chlorophyll

Uses the red-edge band to stay sensitive in dense canopy where NDVI can saturate. Useful for chlorophyll and nitrogen-related patterns.

View on terrain ↑

NDWI — Water content

Highlights water and wetness contrasts in the visible and near-infrared bands. Useful for drainage, inundation, and surface water signals.

View on terrain ↑

NDMI — Canopy moisture

Compares near-infrared and short-wave infrared response. Useful for moisture stress and canopy hydration patterns.

View on terrain ↑

EVI — Enhanced vegetation

Reduces atmospheric and soil-background effects compared with NDVI. Useful when dense vegetation or haze makes simple contrast less stable.

View on terrain ↑

SAVI — Soil-adjusted vegetation

Compensates for exposed soil brightness. Useful for sparse canopy, early growth, dry fields, or mixed bare-soil scenes.

View on terrain ↑

CIre — Chlorophyll index

Expresses near-infrared to red-edge contrast as a chlorophyll-oriented ratio. Useful for within-field nutrition and canopy status variation.
